    What do these results mean

    What is starting wattage and running wattage

    Some appliances require a higher amount of power for a few seconds when starting, once started they may only then need a fraction of that power to run, this is what is know as the 'Running Wattage'. Electrical appliances that have electric motors and moving parts, such as power tools, a furnace or fan will have a higher starting wattage. Other appliance will consistantly require the same power.

    Why do we give 'Recommended Wattage' & 'Maximum Wattage' in the results

    The recommended wattage is calculated based on the highest starting wattage and then accumilated wattages of all other chosen appliances. This guarentees that the size of genertor that is recommended is capable of starting all of your appliances individually and can then comfortably run them. The recommendtion assumes that you will not need to start all your chosen appliances at once. Maximum wattage is the accumilation of all the starting wattages. For most people the recommended wattage is the best size to use.
